# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Church staff and administrators at CCF Eastwood, working across six domains: Members, Guests, Small Groups, Ministries, Events, and Volunteers. Two roles — Super Admin (full access) and Staff (scoped by feature area and event). They use this tool daily, often in a task-focused context: processing registrations, managing group assignments, tracking attendance, reviewing volunteer status. They are not developers. They are people who care about the congregation.

## Product Purpose

An internal admin platform for managing church community operations. Staff use it to track the discipleship pipeline — from a guest attending their first event, to joining a small group, to becoming a member in ministry. Success means staff can move through their work without friction, find what they need quickly, and trust that the data they're looking at is accurate.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, serious, calm. Efficient and professional, but also kind — the people in this system are not just records. The interface should reflect the care that staff bring to their work without becoming sentimental or decorative about it.

## Reference

Asana — structured, task-focused, well-spaced, calm hierarchy. Not flashy, not sterile. Clear affordances, reliable patterns, quiet use of color.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-generated product UI. No hero metrics with giant numbers and gradient accents. No glassmorphism. No identical icon-card grids. No gradient text. No feature that looks like it was assembled from a SaaS template. The interface should not announce itself — it should get out of the way.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity before everything** — Every screen should be immediately legible. Staff shouldn't spend mental energy reading the UI; they should spend it on their work.
2. **Serve the person behind the record** — The data in this system represents real people in a community. The interface should carry that weight quietly — structured and precise, never clinical.
3. **Earn each element** — Nothing decorative, nothing superfluous. If a visual element doesn't help the user make a decision or complete a task, it doesn't belong.
4. **Calm under complexity** — Even the most complex flows — matching algorithms, event registration, breakout assignment — should feel unhurried and structured. Don't let complexity leak into the UI.
5. **Trust through consistency** — Predictable layouts, predictable interactions. The system should never surprise the user about what an action will do.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Text contrast must always meet minimum ratios — foreground on background, muted text on muted backgrounds, text on colored surfaces. No reliance on color alone to convey meaning. Sufficient focus indicators for keyboard navigation.
